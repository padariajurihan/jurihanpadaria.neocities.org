Profile

Origin: Final Fantasy 7

Age: 20 (Final Fantasy 7), 22 (Final Fantasy: Advent Children), 23 (Dirge of Cerberus -Final Fantasy VII)

Height: 167cm (5'6")

Race: Human

Occupation: Bar hostess, Avalanche member.

Personality: Kind, determined, loyal and protective.

Appearance: Tifa Lockhart is characterized by her very dark hair, ranging from brown to black, which typically reaches below her waist and is tied at the tips to create a dolphin-tail split. She possesses red eyes, though they are occasionally depicted as brown, and an athletic build with a slender, toned physique and visible abdominals. Her combat-ready appearance often contrasts with her shy personality

Her signature attire generally consists of a sleeveless tank top that exposes her midriff, a miniskirt, and a single white teardrop earring on her left ear. In the original Final Fantasy VII, this includes a white tank top, black miniskirt with suspenders, and red and black gloves that extend to her elbows. The Remake version of this outfit adds realistic details like military-inspired suspenders, a black sports bra, and a red-and-black leather vambrace featuring Norse runes.

> Other notable physical and clothing details include: She bears a scar across her lower chest from Sephiroth’s attack, though this is not shown on-screen. Tifa also has a "Cowgirl Outfit" which is often seen in flashbacks, this "chocobo rancher" look features a short leather skirt, a button-up corset, a white shirt revealing her belly, and a cowboy hat. In Advent Children, she wears a black zip-up vest over a white tank top with black shorts and a pink ribbon on her left arm in remembrance of Aerith. And lastly but not the least important - depending on player choices, she can wear a violet minidress, a black leopard-print cheongsam with "odango" hair buns, or an exotic short black kimono-style dress

Notable Traits: Motherly figure, Resilience, Empathic Persona, Deadly Combatant and Moral Compass.

Story

> The Roots of a Guardian

Tifa was born in the mountain town of Nibelheim on May 3, 1987. Her childhood was marked by a fragile bond with her neighbor, Cloud Strife; though they were not close initially, their fates became intertwined after the death of Tifa’s mother when she was eight years old. Driven by grief, Tifa attempted to climb Mt. Nibel to find her mother, leading to a near-fatal fall from a bridge that left her in a coma for seven days. During her adolescence, she became the most prized student of the martial arts master Zangan, learning to channel her inner pain into physical discipline. Before Cloud left for Midgar to join SOLDIER, she met him at the town’s water tower, where she made him promise to protect her if she were ever in trouble. This promise became the emotional anchor for her character throughout the series.

> Tragedy and the Resistance

The trajectory of Tifa’s life changed forever during the Nibelheim Incident five years before the main story. After Sephiroth went mad and burned her hometown, Tifa found her father murdered at the Nibel Reactor and attempted to strike down Sephiroth herself, only to be severely wounded. Zangan rescued her and took her to Midgar for life-saving surgery. Carrying the weight of her trauma in silence, she eventually opened the bar Seventh Heaven in the Sector 7 slums, which served as both a sanctuary for others and a secret headquarters for the resistance group Avalanche. When she eventually reunited with Cloud, she noticed his memories were inconsistent but recruited him into Avalanche to keep him close and monitor his fragile state. During their early operations, Tifa acted as the "voice of reason", often feeling deep guilt over the reactor bombings and the innocent lives lost.

> The Global Pursuit and Silent Doubt

Following the destruction of the Sector 7 plate and the rescue of Aerith from Shinra HQ, Tifa joined the party in their worldwide hunt for Sephiroth. Throughout this journey, she remained a stabilizing force, acting as a motherly figure who provided emotional grounding for her allies. However, she struggled with a "silent strength," choosing not to confront Cloud about his fractured identity even when his version of the Nibelheim tragedy contradicted her own. She doubted her own recollections and feared that exposing the truth would cause Cloud to collapse entirely. This tension reached a breaking point when Sephiroth successfully manipulated Cloud into relinquishing the Black Materia, leading to the awakening of the Weapons and the impending threat of Meteor.

> Restoration in the Lifestream

After Cloud went missing and the party was captured by Shinra, Tifa narrowly escaped public execution in Junon. She eventually discovered a catatonic Cloud in Mideel, suffering from severe mako poisoning. When an earthquake sent both of them into the Lifestream, Tifa was finally able to enter Cloud’s subconscious. In this surreal landscape, she used her truest power—her devotion and shared history—to piece his shattered identity back together, revealing that he had indeed been in Nibelheim during the incident as an infantryman. This act of emotional healing allowed the team to reunite with a sense of purpose, leading them to the Northern Cave for the final battle to vanquish Sephiroth and release the magic of Holy.

> A Legacy of Warmth

In the aftermath of Meteor’s defeat, Tifa focused on rebuilding a sense of normalcy in the new city of Edge. She opened a new Seventh Heaven and became a parental figure to Marlene and a young orphan named Denzel, striving to keep her found family together despite Cloud’s subsequent withdrawal into guilt and illness. During the Geostigma crisis, she defended her family from the remnants of Sephiroth, notably engaging in a fierce duel with Loz. Ultimately, Tifa’s story is defined by her transition from a survivor of ash to a "hearth in a world full of storms," proving that her truest strength lies not in her fists, but in her unwavering endurance and kindness.
