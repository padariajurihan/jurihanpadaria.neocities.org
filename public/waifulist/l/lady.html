Profile

Origin: Devil May Cry 3: Dante's Awakening - Special Edition, Devil May Cry 4: Special Edition, Devil May Cry 5: Special Edition

Age:18 (Devil May Cry 3: Dante's Awakening), 33 (Devil May Cry 4: Special Edition), 38 (Devil May Cry 5: Special Edition)

Height: 170 cm (5'7")

Race: Human

Occupation: Devil Hunter

Personality: Her personality is defined by a profound determination and a mission-driven life, shaped largely by the tragic betrayal of her father, Arkham. Her character evolves significantly throughout the franchise, moving from a revenge-obsessed youth to a seasoned, professional devil hunter.

Consistently described as a "fierce woman" with a "no-nonsense attitude". She is an assertive and taciturn warrior who maintains a laser focus on completing her missions. In combat, her mindset is "intense" and "relentless," to the point where she has been described as a "psychotic devil hunting female" due to her lethal efficiency.

Appearance: In Devil May Cry 3, she wore a schoolgirl-style outfit consisting of a short white blouse that exposed her midriff, a skirt made of protective tactical plating, and dark shorts underneath, tall orange-brown boots, and dark brown gloves.

In Devil May Cry 4, in order to reflect a more "adult and professional" persona, her hair grew longer (reaching her collarbone), and she began wearing sleek sunglasses. Her outfit became more daring, featuring a fitted white jacket worn over a harness with no shirt underneath, paired with white shorts and red gloves.

In Devil May Cry 5, she returns to a choppy short bob similar to her original hairstyle. Her primary outfit consisted of a cropped off-white leather jacket over a white long-sleeved blouse, black shorts, and black leg warmers that resemble leggings. After being rescued in the game, she is also briefly seen in a loose, baggy white mechanic's jumpsuit provided by Nico.

Notable Traits: Short Black Hair, Heterochromia, Straight to Action, Fire Weapons Specialist

Story

Devil May Cry 3: Dante's Awakening - Special Edition

Lady (still going by Mary) meets Dante for the first time shortly after his battle with Cerberus at the entrance of the Temen-ni-gru tower. She immediately fires a missile from her Kalina Ann at him; Dante famously dodges it and rides it like a surfboard before the explosion creates a hole she uses to enter the tower.

As she climbs the tower, she is confronted by her father, Arkham, who flips her off a ledge. Dante catches her by the ankle, but she responds by shooting him in the forehead. She manages to stop her fall by jamming her bayonet into the tower wall. This interaction makes her deeply suspicious that Dante is a demon. Later, after Dante bursts out of the Leviathan beast, they are cornered by demons and forced to fight side-by-side. When Dante asks for her name, she refuses to give it, leading him to dismissively call her "Lady" before leaving her to handle the remaining enemies.

Lady finds Dante standing over the seemingly dead body of Arkham. Enraged, she attacks Dante while explaining her father’s sins. Arkham briefly "awakens," blames Vergil for her mother's death, and seemingly dies in her arms, fueling her vow for further revenge. She eventually catches up to Dante and Vergil as they duel. The fight is interrupted by Jester, who reveals he is actually Arkham. He reveals that Lady is a descendant of a human priestess and that her blood is the final key to opening the demon world. He stabs her in the leg with her own weapon to draw her blood and successfully opens the portal.

Refusing to let a demon handle her family matters, she confronts Dante again in the Divine Library. After a fierce battle where Dante defeats her, she finally recognizes his humanity and what he is fighting for. She entrusts him with the Kalina Ann to go into the demon world and stop her father. Once Dante and Vergil defeat Arkham in the demon world, he falls back to Earth in a weakened state. Lady finds him and, despite his pleas for mercy, renounces her birth name and officially adopts the name Lady. She executes Arkham, shooting him four times to avenge her mother.

After the final battle, she finds Dante shedding tears over the loss of his brother. She consoles him by suggesting that somewhere out there, even a "devil may cry" when they lose a loved one. The game ends with the two of them fighting off the remaining demons together, an event that leads to the naming of Dante’s shop.

Devil May Cry 4: Special Edition

Lady visits Dante's shop to inform him and Trish about the Order of the Sword, a small religious congregation in the city of Fortuna. She explains that the Order has recently begun collecting Devil Arms and has even interfered with some of her own jobs, leading her to suspect they have more sinister motives. After Trish takes the Sparda sword and heads to Fortuna ahead of them, Lady follows shortly after. While her specific location during the main gameplay is not shown, it is believed she assisted Trish in evacuating civilians during the crisis.

Once Sanctus and the Savior are defeated, Lady is seen with Dante and Trish observing Nero and Kyrie from a distance. When Dante asks about his pay for the mission, she reassures him that he will be compensated. Later, Lady returns to the shop to pay for Dante's services, providing him with a small roll of money. When Trish complains about the meager amount, Lady justifies it by arguing that the situation in Fortuna only escalated because of Trish's own actions.

The game ends with a customer calling the shop with a password. Despite Dante telling her she won't be paid, Lady forcibly tags along for the new job, expressing excitement about them all working together again.

Devil May Cry 5

Lady is contacted by Morrison and joins Dante and Trish for a mission to confront the demon king Urizen. She and Trish reach the throne room before Dante but are quickly overwhelmed by Urizen’s power. Following Dante's defeat, Urizen captures both women to use their bodies to increase his own strength. For one month, Lady is trapped inside the demon Artemis, serving as its power source. Nero eventually encounters and defeats Artemis, rescuing an unconscious and naked Lady from the demon's core. Nico recognizes her immediately, as Lady was a frequent customer at her weapons shop in Fortuna.

After waking up in Nico’s van, Lady remembers Trish being captured but has no memory of what happened to Dante. She expresses suspicion toward V, questioning if he can be trusted despite Nero’s reassurances. Lady takes an active role in assisting the group, helping Nico clear rubble and eventually driving the van through the roots of the Qliphoth tree. When Dante reappears, Nico "rents" him the Kalina Ann II, a modified version of Lady's signature rocket launcher. Despite initial protests, Lady later pays Nico for the new weapon herself.

As the group escapes the Qliphoth, they find Nero reeling from the discovery that Vergil is his father. Drawing from her own traumatic experience with Arkham, Lady warns him that killing one's own father is a choice from which "you'd never recover". Following the final battle, Lady and Trish are seen in Dante’s office eating pizza and jokingly bickering over who should take over the shop. Morrison arrives to inform them that Dante actually left the deed to the office in his name because he knew "them crazy bitches" would wreck the place. The game ends with Lady and Trish following Morrison to a new job.
