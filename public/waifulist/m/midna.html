Profile

Origin: The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess

Age: 21

Height: Unknown

Race: Twili

Occupation: Devil Hunter

Personality: Midna begins as a mischievous and manipulative figure, initially viewing Link as a "disposable soldier" to help her reclaim her throne. Her cynical demeanor and playful giggling act as a protective facade to mask her deep emotional pain and insecurity. After witnessing Link and Zelda’s selflessness, she transforms into a sincere, caring, and loyal companion. She is a brilliant tactician who displays immense bravery, eventually sacrificing her life to protect her friends and Hyrule. Ultimately, her strong sense of duty leads her to make the selfless choice to sever the link between worlds for the safety of all.

Appearance: In the Imp form, Midna is described as a "puny," dark, and mysterious imp. Her design is characterized by several unique and somewhat "cartoony" features, such as: an oversized head and a slightly "pudgy" body with "chubby exaggerations". These stretched proportions are intended to give her a sense of authority while making her stand out from the more realistic world of Hyrule

Throughout most of the game, she wears a piece of the Fused Shadow as a mask or helmet, which she used to harness the dark magic of her ancestors.

Her true form is that of a tall, beautiful, and regal humanoid woman. Her appearance in this state bears striking similarities to the Gerudo race. She possesses fiery red hair and red eyes. Her overall look is described as "otherworldly," giving off the air of a "foreign princess".

She wears a revealing "half-dress," a cloak, and ornamental forehead jewelry similar to that worn by Gerudo royalty. Her natural form includes an "impossibly complex" interaction between her hair, a hood, and a headpiece.

Notable Traits: Strategic Intelligence, Emotional Resilience, Selfless Devotion, Sense of Duty

Story

> The Origins of the Twilight Princess

Midna's story in The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess is a transformative journey from a displaced, cynical princess to a selfless hero who saves two worlds. Originally the ruler of the Twilight Realm, she was usurped by a servant named Zant, who had been empowered by the dark magic of Ganondorf. Zant placed a curse on Midna, transforming her into a "puny" imp and stripping her of her natural magical abilities. Horrified and seeking a way to reclaim her throne, she fled to Hyrule and sought out the Fused Shadows, ancient relics capable of countering Zant’s power.

> The first encounter

Her role begins as a manipulative companion when she discovers Link in his wolf form within the sewers of Hyrule Castle. Recognizing him as the "Divine Beast" mentioned in Twili legend, she decides to use him as a "servant" to help her retrieve the missing pieces of the Fused Shadow. Initially, Midna acts with pure self-interest, viewing Link as a "disposable soldier" and mocking Princess Zelda while showing little concern for the fate of the Light World.

> With a new heart, comes a new determination

The major turning point occurs after the duo successfully collects the Fused Shadows, only to be ambushed by Zant, who steals the relics and leaves Midna on the brink of death after exposing her to lethal light. Link risks everything to save her, leading to a pivotal moment where Princess Zelda sacrifices her own life force to revive Midna. These acts of selflessness cause Midna to undergo a profound change of heart, dropping her manipulative facade and vowing to protect Hyrule alongside her own realm.

> The Last Battle

Together, Link and Midna repair the Mirror of Twilight, the only link between their dimensions, and invade the Palace of Twilight to confront Zant. After defeating Zant, Midna utilizes the power of the Fused Shadows to finish him off. The story reaches its climax at Hyrule Castle, where Midna sacrifices her life in an attempt to destroy Ganondorf and ensure the safety of Link and Zelda.

> See you later, My Twilight Queen!

Following Ganondorf's ultimate defeat, the Light Spirits revive Midna, finally breaking her curse and restoring her to her true, regal form. In her final act, she fulfills her sense of duty by shattering the Mirror of Twilight with a single tear, severing the link between worlds forever to prevent future conflict, despite the emotional pain of leaving Link behind.
